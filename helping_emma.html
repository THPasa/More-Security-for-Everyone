Ransomware: Every click could be your last

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software (malware) that encrypts the victim's files or locks them out of their system, then demands payment (ransom) for the decryption key or to regain access. Typically, ransomware is delivered through phishing emails, malicious websites, or vulnerabilities in software.Once executed, ransomware can spread across a network, causing widespread disruption, data loss, and financial damage. It is a highly lucrative crime for cybercriminals, making it a constant threat to both individuals and organizations. Take a Moment to Think

After Emma's experience, she's now aware of how quickly things can escalate. The key lessons to remember to stay safe are:

  • Ensure Regular Backups

    The most effective defense against ransomware is a proper backup system. Regular backups ensure that even if an attack happens, you can restore your files without paying a ransom.

  • Never Click on Suspicious Links

    The best way to avoid ransomware is not to click on any suspicious email links. Always verify the sender, especially if the email seems urgent or too good to be true.

  • Stay Up to Date with Cybersecurity Training

    Continuously educating yourself about cybersecurity best practices and emerging threats helps you spot and prevent attacks. Whether through formal training, webinars, or self-study, staying informed is key to protecting your data.

  • Research Before Taking Action

    If you're ever in doubt, research before acting. Consult trusted sources, like your organization's IT team, or verify emails and links before clicking.
