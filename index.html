Social Engineering -- As Strong As the Weakest Link in the Chain


Social engineering is a cyber-attack method based on manipulating human psychology to gain access to sensitive information. Instead of overcoming technical challenges, hackers directly exploit human weaknesses to achieve their goals.

Social engineering attacks have increased rapidly in the last 10 years. Millions of people, from large companies to individual users, have been deceived by this method. Phishing, fake call centers, and deepfake technologies are among the most common methods of these attacks.

As years go by, technology advances and security measures tighten. Yet, billion-dollar losses continue to rise. There's only one explanation: Every security has a vulnerability; the vulnerability of cybersecurity is the HUMAN FACTOR.
